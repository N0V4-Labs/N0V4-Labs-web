Personal blog by Colin MacRitchie

I'm Colin MacRitchie, also known as Shaikko. I build systems that have to work at scale—engines, tools, infrastructure. This is where I write about the decisions that come out of that work: the tradeoffs, the surprises, the things that looked good on paper but fell apart under load.

Most of it is technical. Some of it is just thinking out loud about why certain problems are harder than they seem, or why the obvious solution doesn't actually solve it. If you're interested in low-level design, mechanical sympathy, or the kind of engineering that requires you to care about cache lines and pointer lifetimes, you'll probably find something useful here.
